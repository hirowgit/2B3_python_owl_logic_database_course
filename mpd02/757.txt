Hepatocellular carcinoma in a free-living marmoset (Callithrix sp.) with
concomitant biliary trematodiasis.

Hepatocellular carcinoma (HCC) is rare in New World primates. We report the
gross, microscopical, and immunocytochemical features of a spontaneous HCC in a
free-living marmoset (Callithrix sp.). Hepatitis B and C virus and aflatoxin
immunohistochemistry were negative; however, concomitant intra- and extrahepatic 
biliary trematodiasis could have played a role.
